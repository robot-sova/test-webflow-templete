WHAT IS YOUR SERVICE FEE?

Our service fee is $89. It covers the diagnostics, as well as the time and travel needed in order to bring a certified technician straight to your doorstep.

The pricing for labor is based on industry standards. Labor costs vary based on the type of appliance and the type of repair needed. For most repairs, expect to pay between $220 and $350 plus tax. More complicated repairs can cost as high as $600 or more.

Yes. The tech will diagnose you appliance to find the root of the issue. At the end of the diagnostics, you will be provided with a quote for the labor & parts. If you choose to complete the repair with us, the service call fee will be applied to the cost of repair.

WHAT TYPE OF WARRANTY DO YOU OFFER?

The repair work is covered by a 90 day warranty. Warranty for parts is the same as the manufacturers warranty.

WHICH BRANDS DO YOU SERVICE?

DOES APPLIANCE SERVICE PRO PURCHASE APPLIANCES, USED OR NEW?

No. Appliance Service Pro does not purchase any appliances. We only do repair work.

HOW DO I SCHEDULE AN APPOINTMENT?

In order to schedule a service appointment, you can give us a call, text, email or contact us through our Schedule Service form on our website. You will need to provide your full name, address, phone number, as well as the brand & model number of your appliance.

HOW DO YOU DETERMINE YOUR PRICES?

All our prices are determined according to industry standards. WHEN IS IT TIME TO GET MY APPLIANCE REPAIRED?

Household appliances play a significant role in our everyday life. No one wants to live without their washing machine, stove or refrigerator, even if it is just a few days. What is the average life expectancy of a refrigerator?

The life expectancy of a refrigerator is about 13 years. This means that the average refrigerator will last for about 13 years before needing to be replaced. What are the signs of a failing refrigerator?

There are a few signs that your refrigerator may be failing. First, you may notice that the unit isn't cooling as well as it used to. Another sign is if you notice that your energy bills have increased significantly; this could be due to the fridge having to work harder to maintain a consistent temperature. Lastly, if you hear strange noises coming from the fridge, that's definitely a cause for concern! These could be indications that the compressor or evaporator fan is failing, which can lead to expensive repairs down the road.
